# Governor / Lieutenant Governor Joint Ticket Reference

States where the Governor and Lt. Governor run as a joint ticket (like President/VP) vs. separately elected.

**Database usage**: Use `elections.linked_election_id` to pair Gov ↔ Lt. Gov elections. Only link elections in years *after* a state adopted joint tickets — earlier races were separate.

## Joint Ticket States (26, becoming 27 with AZ in 2026)

Three types:
- **Type A**: Gov nominee selects running mate *after* primary
- **Type B**: Gov candidate selects running mate *before* primary (run as slate)
- **Type C**: Separate primaries, winners run as joint ticket in general

## Separately Elected States (17)

Gov and Lt. Gov appear on the ballot independently — different vote totals, no ticket linkage.

AL, AR, CA, DE, GA, ID, LA, MS, MO, NV, NC, OK, RI, TX, VT, VA, WA

Notable:
- **OK**: Voters rejected joint ticket amendment (SQ 798) in 2018
- **ID**: Lt Gov McGeachin issued executive orders while Gov was out of state (2021)
- **MS**: Still separate as of 2023 election despite some reports otherwise
- **VT**: 2-year terms
